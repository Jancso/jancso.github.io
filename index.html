Welcome to my website!

I'm a second-year master's student in Informatics at the University of Zurich. I received my bachelor's degree in Computational Linguistics and General Linguistics in January 2018 from the University of Zurich.

Besides my studies, I'm working part-time as a student (research) assistant for the ACQDIV and the s.e.a.l. group. You can find my full CV here.

As you might have guessed from my CV, I have interests in various areas. When I began studying at the university, I saw my strengths in linguistics because I could quickly grasp and memorize language grammars. However, I also wanted to try out something more technical and therefore also chose computational linguistics where I learned programming for the first time. Immediately, I was intrigued that I could fully automate certain tasks that I had done manually previously. Eventually, I decided to switch to CS for my master's.

Funnily enough, CS and linguistics have more in common than one would expect, as also evidenced by the number of terms that CS borrowed from linguistics: language, grammar, word, syntax, semantics, etc. In fact, it was the famous linguist Noam Chomsky who created a hierarchy of formal languages, known as the Chomsky-Hierarchy. Now, I have to admit that I find studying formal languages far less interesting than natural ones. Generally, theoretical informatics is not my cup of tea! One the other hand, I'm very interested in the following CS areas (no particular order):

  • Software Engineering & Architecture
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Computational Science
  • Cyber Security

Currently, I'm working in the areas of software engineering (DevOps) and natural language processing (corpus building & processing). Previously, I also worked in the area of bioinformatics (NER of biomedical entities). You can read all about my programming projects here.

Even though, linguistics is not part of my curriculum anymore, (natural) languages still interest me. I'm about to complete my third language course at the university: Russian. Before that, I took Latin and Arabic lessons.

In my free time, I enjoy playing the piano. I mostly play classical pieces from the Baroque, Classical and Romantic era, but have also played more 'contemporary' works such as Debussy's Doctor Gradus ad Parnassum or Joplin's Maple Leaf Rag. I especially like virtuoso and dramatic piano works from the romantic era such as Chopin's Étude Op. 10, No. 4 or Schumann's Piano Sonata No. 2, Mov. 1.

My second hobby is hiking. I especially like T3-T4 hikes in the Glarus and Grisons Alps (Glarner & Büdner Alpen), e.g. Vorder Glärnisch or Lej da la Tscheppa. Usually, you find me hiking in the Glarus Alps since they are quite close to where I live. (By the way, the picture with the mountains was taken close to the Albula Pass)
